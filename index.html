Document A: The Vision Document

This document, which includes the scenario, rationale, and success criteria, is often called a Product Requirements Document (PRD) or a Product Vision Document. It's the foundational document that defines what the product is, why it's needed, and how its success will be measured. It's the "what" and "why" of the project.

Essential! ‚ú®

Document B: The Design and Task Plan

This is commonly known as a Technical Design Document or Project Plan. It's the "how" of the project. It outlines the specific tasks needed to build the product and provides a high-level overview of the product's architecture and design. The "record of tasks" might also be part of a separate Project Gantt Chart or Work Breakdown Structure.

Document C: The Implementation Plan

This document, focused on "developing the product," is a Technical Specification Document. It goes into the nitty-gritty details of how the product will be built, including specific technologies, coding standards, and architectural decisions. It's the blueprint for the development team.

Document D: The Final Product

This document is a User Manual or User Guide. It describes how the completed product functions from the user's perspective. It explains the features, how to use them, and what the user can expect. Think of it as the instruction manual for the final app.

Document E: The Future Roadmap

This is the Product Roadmap or Post-Launch Analysis. It looks beyond the initial release. It covers the product's future, including potential improvements, new features (extensibility), and strategies for growth (marketing opportunities). This document helps a team think strategically about the product's evolution.

Professional Formatting

Each document is professionally formatted with clear sections, proper headings, and industry-standard structure. Perfect for presenting to stakeholders, investors, or development teams.
